Discover the cardiac coherence

Cardiac coherence is a breathing technique that aims to immediately decrease the heart rate in a particularly stressful situation.

You can be fully involved in the regulation of your emotions in just 15 minutes a day.

What is it ?

It is a method like a “physiological balance” that comes to regulate the heart rate with our emotions.

It aims to practice voluntary breathing, which will allow soothing of anxiety.

The heart will then echo the breath, harmonizing with it.

It is at the intersection between relaxation and biofeedback (which is the rehabilitation of behavior through awareness of physiological reactions).

The main objective of practicing cardiac coherence is to bring about a rapid calming.



In a global way, it will allow a better management of its emotions, consequently to decrease, in a notable way, daily stress and anxiety.

The immediate effects of cardiac coherence last approximately 3 to 6 hours.

Health Benefits

Cardiac coherence can be used by everyone.
It can be used by children, adolescents, adults and the elderly, even those with certain physical or cognitive difficulties.


  • Useful in anxiogenic situations
  • Help regulate the heartrate
  • Decrease in cortisol (hormone secreted during chronic stress)
  • Create more dopamine and serotonin (pleasure and anxiety-reducing hormones
  • Reduce risk of depression
  • Loss of weight
  • Balance of the nervous system
  • Reduction in attention disorders
  • Improve the quality of sleep
  • Positive developments in memory, concentration and learning
  • Improvement of concentration
  • Improved digestion
  • Decrease in pain in the case of irritable bowel syndrome

It can also be recommended for people at risk of heart problems, to prevent certain heart diseases as high blood pressure or risk of cardiovascular accidents.

How is it working ?

It's very simple!

Just find a quiet place where you won't be disturbed and sit down, on a chair or a floor, with your back straight.

A breath is composed of an inhalation and an exhalation. Each of them must last 5 seconds.

For this you can use a watch, an application or the animation below.

It is advisable to use abdominal breathing and therefore to breathe through the belly. It is possible to breathe through the nose and/or the mouth. Find your way to practice cardiac coherence. The important thing is that you feel good.

In practice

Breathe in as the petals grow (5 seconds).

Breathe out as the petals shrink (5 seconds).
